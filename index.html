Mission Statement:

The Solar Thermal Generator Project is an attempt to harness the solar energy the earth receives every day and convert that into electrical energy with the use of a solid state device. The way this project differs from photovoltaic energy is that the sun provides only heat and no chemical reaction is needed. The TEG chip uses the Seebeck Effect of dissimilar metal junctions and temperature gradients to produce electric current.

Project Objectives:

Solar Collection:

The method chosen for collecting and focusing the solar radiation is a parabolic reflector. The nature of the parabolic shape provides a fine point for the total energy collected per square inch of dish. This beam will focus on a “hot plate” designed to evenly distribute the heat to the TEG modules. The dish size should collect one-third of the power spread over a square meter. This should be about 350 Watts.
